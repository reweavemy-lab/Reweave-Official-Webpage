The Heart of Reweave

Our artisans are the soul of Reweave. Each with their own unique story and specialized craft, they bring generations of knowledge and skill to every piece they create. By choosing Reweave, you're directly supporting these talented individuals and helping preserve cultural traditions that might otherwise be lost.

Priya Collective

location_on Gujarat, India
Specialty: Hand Block Printing & Natural Dyeing

The Priya Collective is a women-led cooperative of 35 artisans specializing in traditional block printing techniques using natural dyes. Founded in 2010, the collective has revitalized ancient printing methods while providing sustainable livelihoods for women in rural Gujarat.

"Each print tells a story of our heritage. When I create, I feel connected to my grandmother and all the women who came before me. This is not just our livelihood—it's our legacy."
— Meena, Lead Artisan

Traditional Techniques

Our artisans employ ancient techniques that have been refined over generations, creating pieces that are not only beautiful but also culturally significant.

Block Printing

Origin: India, 12th Century

A method of printing patterns on textiles using carved wooden blocks. Each color requires a separate block, and the process demands precision and patience. Natural dyes are created from plants, minerals, and insects.

Backstrap Loom Weaving

Origin: Mesoamerica, Pre-Columbian Era

A portable loom that attaches to the weaver's body, allowing for intricate control over tension and pattern. This ancient technique creates textiles with complex geometric designs and symbolic motifs.

Ikat

Origin: Multiple regions, dating back centuries

A resist-dyeing technique where yarns are bound and dyed before weaving, creating patterns with characteristic feathered edges. Requires meticulous planning and mathematical precision.

Kantha Embroidery

Origin: Bengal region, 1st Century

A running stitch technique traditionally used to repurpose old saris into quilts. The simple stitch creates texture and patterns, often telling stories through motifs and designs.
